The CTF

The Root-Me 10k CTF was organized to celebrate the 10k members on the discord of Root-Me. It was a fun CTF with interesting challenges. The categories were : OSINT, Misc, Web, Forensic, Pwn, Reverse, Programming, Intro and Crypto. I managed to get to the 18th place.

The challenge

The OSINT challenge was to find the name and the call sign of the boat on the picture below.

Here’s the description of the challenge :

Write-Up

exif data ?

So the first thing I’ve done is to check the image with exiftool to see if there’s nothing helpful.

We can see that the picture has a creator, “Rene Te Pas” and a creation date.

On to google

I reverse search the image on google and yandex but I found nothing that useful. So I looked up “Rene Te Pas” and a boat appeared in the results. The boat came from a photographer called “Rene Te Pas” on a site named “MarineTraffic”.

Needless to say that the biggest mistake I made on this challenge is to tunnel-vision on this website. I thought I had everything right : The right photographer, the fact that many boats that the Netherland flag on them, etc. The website was not the right one…

So I came back to the search results, and I found a website similar to MarineTraffic called “VesselFinder” and as I expected, there is indeed a photographer called “Rene Te Pas”. After a bit of research on the website, this is what I found :

The name is Our Roots II, now we have to find the call sign. For that, I searched the boat on MarineTraffic and I found it from a different photographer :
